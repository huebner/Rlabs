Rcode for inference for means (t-interval or t-test)

The production of a nationally marketed detergent results in certain workers receiving prolonged exposure to the bacillus subtilis enzyme. Nineteen workers were tested to determine the effects of these exposures on various respiratory functions. The airflow rate, FEV1, is the ratio of a person’s forced expiratory volume to the vital capacity, VC (max. volume of air a person can exhale after taking a deep breath). If the enzyme has an effect, it will be to reduce the FEV1/VC ratio. The norm is 0.80 in persons with no lung dysfunction.

ratio <- c(0.61, 0.7, 0.63, 0.76, 0.67, 0.72, 0.64, 0.82, 0.88, 0.82, 0.78, 
    0.84, 0.83, 0.82, 0.74, 0.85, 0.73, 0.85, 0.87)

Note that to get a t interval and t test the same function is used. Type

?t.test

to check what options are available.

A 90% confidence interval can be obtained with

t.test(ratio, mu = 0.8, conf.level = 0.9)

However the research question is whether the ratio is lower than normal. This is a one sided hypothesis test, namely the alternative is \( H_A: \mu < 0.80. \)

temp.1sided <- t.test(ratio, mu = 0.8, alternative = "less")

The pvalue is 0.0523. The data provide evidence that exposure to B. subtilis may reduce the FEV1/VC ratio, but are inconclusive at the 5% significance level.
